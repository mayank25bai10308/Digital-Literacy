# 🛡️ 'Stay Safe Online' Checklist for College Students (India)

A digital safety guide to protecting your data, identity, and finances while navigating campus life and the internet.

---

### 💳 Financial & UPI Safety
1.  **Never Enter a PIN to Receive Money:** Scammers often send "Request Money" links via apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm. Remember: You only enter your UPI PIN to **send** or **pay** money, never to receive it.
2.  **Verify UPI IDs Before Confirming:** Always double-check the recipient's name that appears after scanning a QR code at local vendors or when paying for shared student resources.

### 🔐 Account & Password Security
3.  **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your university email, social media, and banking apps. Use an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) rather than just SMS for better security.
4.  **Use Unique Passwords for Every Site:** Avoid using the same password for your college portal and your personal email. Use a trusted Password Manager to keep track of complex, unique credentials.

### 🌐 Browsing & Public Network Safety
5.  **Avoid Campus 'Open' Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your bank account or enter credit card details while connected to free, unsecured public Wi-Fi. Use your mobile data or a VPN if necessary.
6.  **Check for 'HTTPS' and Padlocks:** Before entering any details on a website, ensure the URL starts with `https://`. This is especially important for results appearing in "Student Discount" or "Cheap Textbook" searches.

### 📱 Social Media & Privacy
7.  **Limit Personal Info on Social Media:** Do not post photos of your ID cards, admit cards, or hostel room numbers. These can be used for identity theft or physical "doxing."
8.  **Beware of "Urgent" Messages from Friends:** If a friend "DMs" you asking for an urgent loan or an OTP they "accidentally" sent to your number, call them directly to verify. Their account may have been hacked.

---

### 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime in India
If you have been a victim of financial fraud or any cybercrime, act immediately:

* **Official Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline Number:** **1930** (Call this immediately for financial frauds to increase the chances of freezing the stolen funds).

> **Pro-Tip:** Report any incident within the "Golden Hour" (the first 2 hours) to maximize the possibility of recovering lost money.
