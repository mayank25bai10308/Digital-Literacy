# 📱 Social Media Checklist for College Students

Managing your digital footprint is just as important as maintaining your GPA. Use this checklist to ensure your online presence works for you, not against you.

---

### ✅ The Do's
* **Audit your privacy settings:** Check these at least once a semester. Platforms update their terms frequently, and you should know exactly who can see your photos.
* **Build a professional LinkedIn:** Use a clean headshot and list your relevant coursework, projects, and volunteer experience.
* **Think before you "Like":** Remember that your activity (likes and comments) is often visible to others. Only engage with content you'd be comfortable defending.
* **Showcase your wins:** Use your platforms to share academic milestones, internships, or creative hobbies that show you are a well-rounded individual.
* **Google yourself:** Periodically search your own name in an incognito window to see what recruiters will find when they inevitably do the same.

---

### ❌ The Don'ts
* **Don't post illegal activity:** Avoid any photos or text referencing underage drinking, drug use, or other illicit acts.
* **Don't "rage-post":** Never vent about professors, classmates, or your current employer. This is often flagged as a red flag for "toxic" behavior.
* **Don't overshare PII:** Avoid posting your student ID, birthdate, home address, or specific daily schedule (for personal safety).
* **Don't engage with trolls:** Getting into heated, public arguments can make you look unprofessional, regardless of who started it.
* **Don't forget the "Grandma Rule":** If you wouldn't want your grandmother or a future hiring manager to see it, it doesn't belong on the internet.

---

> **Pro-Tip:** Your digital footprint is permanent. Even "deleted" or "expired" content (like Snaps or Stories) can be captured via screenshot. When in doubt, leave it out.
